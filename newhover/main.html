Harm Reduction in Harlem

By Laura Duclos

In 2016, opioids were involved in 44% of overdose deaths in 2016, a 28% increase from 2015, according to the NYC Department of Health. Fentanyl, a synthetic and fast-acting opiate is face of the opioid epidemic and most commonly present in heroin and cocaine-involved deaths. The drug is known to have potency 50 to 100 times the strength of morphine.

Despite the rising trend of fentanyl-related deaths, users are still seeking new ways to combine the opiate with the other drugs, causing dangerous and sometimes lethal reactions. "What people are doing now is giving themselves a double dose," said David Cornish, a field coordinator with Harlem United said. "A lot of clients are on methadone, and when they are on methadone they're more likely to use heroin, which could be laced with fentanyl. And the fentanyl and methadone together is like a deadly dosage. "

Harlem United is a nonprofit harm reduction center that was created over three decades ago in the height of the AIDS crisis, now places itself on the frontline in the battle of the nationally-spread opioid epidemic. Although some of the services the organizations provide skate on the line of legality, the outreach team believes the key to helping drug abusers is consistency to earn the trust of drug users.
